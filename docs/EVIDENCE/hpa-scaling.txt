Proof: HPA scaling under real load
====================================

Observed during normal operation (memory usage exceeded the 75% target
after the app had been running under organic + load-test traffic):

$ kubectl get hpa -n taskapp
NAME      REFERENCE            TARGETS                          MINPODS   MAXPODS   REPLICAS   AGE
backend   Deployment/backend   cpu: 1%/60%, memory: 123%/75%    2         6         6          2d3h

- memory usage at 123% of the 75% target triggered the HPA to scale
  backend from its baseline 2 replicas up to the configured maximum of 6.
- Confirms HorizontalPodAutoscaler is live and reacting to real resource
  pressure, not just present as unused YAML.
- See also docs/EVIDENCE/zero-downtime-rollout.log for a rolling update
  performed while the Deployment was scaled to 6 replicas (HPA-managed),
  proving the rollout logic and HPA interact correctly together.
